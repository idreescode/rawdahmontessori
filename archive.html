The Rawḍah Montessori

The Rawdah literally means the Garden which is the best way to describe where we educate our students. Our building is part of the English Heritage and is surrounded by beautiful landscaped gardens.

Our education vision is to help our students to succeed beyond their expectations. We aim to personalise their learning and achieve success through motivation and self-directed learning. We place character development and faith at the heart of our endeavours. Our long term aspiration is to help our students to become beacons of light in their communities.

TRM education is underpinned by Montessori teaching and learning methods.

Mission Statement

The Rawdah Montessori aims to become an outstanding independent school which nurtures young people to aim high and work hard, academically and spiritually, to lead and serve in their communities. Our holistic, Montessori-inspired curriculum prepares students to be well-rounded, autonomous, and collaborative learners and creative thinkers. Continuing the rich heritage of education on our campus, within a caring community firmly grounded in faith, our secondary program will offer opportunities for pupils to focus on technology, innovation & enterprise, religious studies, and the arts; while a full range of clubs and activities will enrich our pupils’ skills and experience, from martial arts to gardening.

1:1 & SMALL GROUP TUITION

Our teaching model is not didactic like conventional schools, where teachers stand at the front and explain concepts to a class.

We do not have large classroom type lessons in The Rawdah.

Students have 1:1 and small group tuition regularly on concepts they find challenging. All of our students will attend regularly lectures throughout the day as part of their self-directed learning programme. Our multi-age setting also supports our students to learn from each other.

The curriculum and resources are accessible on The Rawdah cloud learning management system which we have uniquely built for our students. Learning is therefore accessible at home and throughout the holiday periods. We also have textbooks and print out for students to reduce prolonged screen times.

Enterprise

All students at the College will learn how to do a start up small businesses as personal projects under the guidance of mentors. Students will gain experience in developing new ideas for market testing, how to use technology to propel their vision, learn how to raise finance and manage budgets, and understand the strategies they will need to make the business successful.

Emotional Well-Being & Happiness

The emotional well-being and happiness of our students is our primary concern. At The Rawdah we ensure our students are not experiencing pressures of constant tests, mass assessments and competitiveness in terms of grading.

We believe that these pressures are not good for children and their development. Assessment data and grades are personal to the student. Their progress is determined by their interests and efforts and working at a pace which matches their cognitive ability. There should be no pressures and rivalry between the learners, our multi age setting encourages children to learn from one another on a regular basis.

Faith, Citizenship & Lifestyles

The Rawdah spirituality and ethos is based on Abrahamic beliefs, traditions and teachings as explained in the last monotheistic religion, Islam. Our students also learn about other world religions, such as Christianity & Judaism. We have first class prayer facilities located onsite in a beautiful gothic building which the students can use throughout the day for reflection.

Students are encouraged to be balanced in their characters, to avoid extremities, embrace diversity and learn how to respect and tolerate opinions which may differ to theirs. Students also learn about their British heritage, values and history.

In terms of lifestyles, we encourage our students to eat healthy, grow their own fruit and vegetables onsite, avoid sugary and processed snacks and fizzy drinks. Students have access to drinking water from fountains in education spaces. All of our students are involved in games and sport.
